# Your Life is at Stake

You are losing 5–10 healthy years, and living a stupider, sicker, poorer, less happy life than you should.

- **Shorter:** US healthy, non-disabled, life expectancy now runs ~10 years below Australia, Korea, and Japan.[^9]
- **Stupider:** Measured IQ has fallen ~1.8 points per decade across developed nations since the 1970s — the "reverse Flynn effect" — after a century of gains.[^2]
- **Sicker:** Colorectal cancer in adults under 50 has roughly doubled since the early 1990s and is now the leading cancer killer of men under 50.[^3] US obesity climbed from ~14% in 1980 to ~42% in 2020.[^4] We can go on and on here.
- **Impotent:** Roughly 1 in 4 men newly presenting with erectile dysfunction is under 40,[^5] and male sperm counts fell ~52% globally between 1973 and 2018.[^6]
- **Poorer:** US per-capita healthcare spending is roughly 4x its 1990 level in real terms.[^7]
- **Less happy:** US teen depression prevalence roughly doubled between 2005 and 2020.[^8]

A "crazy" idea: maybe 0.5% of your brain being plastic makes you less smart. Maybe we can explain the majority of our health problems from the space age pollution we receive from conception. 

Perhaps, after we are 100% certain we have ruled out the foundation for biological health we can turn to social discussions about tech and education.

## The World won't Save You

Your tap water is not designed for your health — it's designed to not kill you... today. Your food is designed to hijack your taste buds at minimum cost, not to nourish you. Your air isn't designed at all.

You live sandwiched between short-sighted institutions doing the minimum for your health and companies doing the maximum to hijack you. This is the post-WWII inflection we've been living inside since the 1970s, when subsidized industrialization turned food and consumer goods into chemistry experiments at scale.

Assume everything is poisonous, because statistically it is:

- **PFAS** ("forever chemicals") sit in the blood of ~98% of Americans[^10] and appear in ~45% of US tap water samples.[^11]
- **Microplastics** have been found in 100% of placentas tested,[^12] ~80% of human blood,[^13] and brain concentrations rose ~50% between 2016 and 2024 — roughly 0.5% of a modern brain is now plastic by mass.[^14]
- **99% of the planet** breathes air exceeding WHO pollution limits.[^15]
- **BPA** is detected in ~93% of Americans over age 6.[^16]

And it's accelerating — global plastic production is on track to **triple by 2060**.[^14] And... plastic is just the canary. You're inside a chemistry experiment that nobody ran safety trials for. There are ~80,000+ chemicals in US commerce and only a few hundred have been tested for health effects under the original TSCA.[^18] The US bans ~11 cosmetic ingredients; the EU bans 1,300+.[^19]

From conception infants are fed plastic and chemicals through the placenta, breastmilk, and home air. They are swaddled in plastic sheets, pillows, clothes, and grow up consuming contaminated food, air, and water. 

Pollution is a global challenge because we share the same fishbowl. The rocket in the US damages the ozone for Australia. The clouds of micro plastic in China rain down over the oceans polluting fish exported to the United States.

As we all compete with each other industries take shortcuts -- it's a classic Tragedy of the Commons situation. A global conspiracy to NOT pollute will not work due to the Game Theory. If you are a nation and you can grow fast by polluting you will because you will only feel a tiny fraction of that pollution. You can easily export your pollution to the oceans and to the air which no one nation can own.

So no, the world isn't going to keep you safe, it's too busy, chasing short term nonsense.

## Longevity is Excess

Longevit isn't deprivation, it's extreme excess, such that when something bad happens you can shrug it off. 

Consider a "competitive" company and a monopoly. The monopoly has longevity because they have such excess that they can invest, they can lose a few rounds, make a few mistakes, and keep on going. The competitive company cutting costs is always living at the edge, barely getting by. They don't look ahead to the future, they are trapped in the present making local choice after local choice until they hit a dead end and keel over.

The sick don't survive the first pneumonia, the first car accident, or the first hard year at work. But those with a surplus of extra muscle, extra mitochondrial capacity, extra microbial diversity, and extra cognitive reserve do. Top-quartile VO2max roughly halves your all-cause mortality. Muscle mass above the median roughly halves ICU death. That's what excess capacity buys you.

To build our longevity, we have to build a resevoir of capacity and that means longevity is about maximizing all the goodness of life under the curve. It means performing at peak levels until the day you die.
