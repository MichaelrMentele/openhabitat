## Problem

<Image src={waterContaminants} alt="Illustration of a water glass contaminated with arsenic, germs, nitrates, rocket fuel, lead and copper, and disinfection byproducts." />

The core problem is that municipal water treatment was designed to prevent acute illness (cholera, dysentery) — it's optimized for not killing you today, not for minimizing chronic low-dose exposure over decades.

This chronic exposure to forever chemicals, metals, and plastics won't kill you today, but it will make you tireder, stupider, sicker, cancerous, and demented over time.

**Heavy metals** (lead, arsenic, cadmium, mercury, chromium-6) are the big ones. They bioaccumulate — your body doesn't efficiently excrete them, so low-level chronic exposure builds up over years. Lead is neurotoxic at any concentration (there's no safe level). Arsenic is a carcinogen. These leach from pipes, solder joints, and natural geology. Austin confirmed no lead service lines in 2024, but that doesn't rule out lead solder in pre-1986 homes or brass fixtures.

**Disinfection byproducts** (THMs, haloacetic acids) are the tradeoff of chlorination. Austin uses chloramine, which reacts with organic matter in the water to form compounds like bromodichloromethane and chloroform. These are associated with increased cancer risk and reproductive issues. Austin's levels are within legal limits but 100–200x above EWG's health-based guidelines.

**PFAS** ("forever chemicals") don't break down in the environment or in your body. They accumulate in organs and blood, and are linked to cancer, thyroid disruption, immune suppression, and reproductive harm. Austin's levels tested low under UCMR 5, but detection limits and testing frequency are still evolving.

**Microplastics and pharmaceuticals** are emerging concerns that aren't well-regulated or routinely tested. Municipal treatment wasn't designed to remove them.

**Minerals at excess** — not toxic per se, but Austin has hard water (calcium, magnesium) that affects everything downstream.

## Goal

Ideal water is water from a pristine mountain channel. Rich with minerals, with no contaminants.

The ideal would be all the water you use for drinking and for bathing is like this.

## Gap

1. You cannot rely on the city. Governments have little incentive to care about your optimal biological health — they care about meeting compliance levels.
2. Compliance levels are often NOT met. Austin city water has 42 contaminants above federal requirements including disinfectants, forever chemicals, and plastics.
3. Compliance levels are far below optimal.
4. We have no idea what is actually coming into our body. Miles of pipe and pump stations sit between the treatment plant and your tap. These pipes are often decades old — or, worse, brand new and still leaching material into the water.

## Solution

A two-tier model:

1. Whole-home reverse osmosis and remineralization
2. Drinking water only (under-sink RO)

Reverse osmosis uses a membrane to filter over 99% of remaining particulate — heavy metals, DBPs, PFAS, and microplastics.
