There are two lotteries that determine our lives:
1. the Genetic Lottery
2. the Geographical Lottery

Of these, genetics is the less important force for two reasons:
1. it averages out, for most of us it's not an advantage or disadvantage
2. we cannot change it for decades to come

For these reasons we should discard it's consideration and focus on solving the Geographical Lottery.

## Solving the Exposome Solves the Lottery

You are a product of your environment. Every input — the people you model, the air you breathe, the water you drink, the light you see, the fabrics against your skin — is part of your **exposome**: the sum total of everything that has ever touched you since the moment of conception.

While our starting point is fixed, our ending point is not. We can move, we can change our social circle, and we can shape our environment so that it helps us -- instead of works against us. 

Further, if we can turn habitats into code, then we can provide every human on the planet with an optimal health environment. This in turn, reduces the scope of the Geographical Lottery, into a Social Lottery, which is a step in the right direction.

## The Vision

A perfect exposome for everyone — built from a perfect habitat, designed once, released openly, free for anyone to copy.

OpenHabitat is that blueprint. Every system, every space, every product decision worked out in the open so the design for an optimal environment spreads as fast as the threats do. Not a luxury good sold to the wealthy but the default setting of every home. 

Habitat is the half of OpenHabitat that addresses this directly. Your immediate environment is the part of the Geographical Lottery you can actually redesign. This tab walks through the systems and spaces that let you do it.
