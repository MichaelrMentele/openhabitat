## Problem

The most important space in your house is the bedroom.

Why? 
1. A great day starts the night before and
2. Sleep is where all the gains are made:
- Learning: You do not actually learn during the day, you learn at night
- Recovery: Your muscles and tissues don't grow during the day, they grow when you rest
- Pollution: the bedroom is where you are most vulnerable for longest.

In short, poor sleep is equivalent to poor performance in everything and thus a poorer life.

Sadly, about half get poor sleep and the vast majority are not getting optimal sleep.

## Goal

Our goal for the bedroom, besides cuddles and intimacy, is perfect sleep. We worked hard studying and training and we want all of our benefits. We want to wake up in peak performance.

Targets for perfect sleep:

| Measure | Method |
| --- | --- |
| REM | Wearable |
| Deep Sleep | Wearable |
| Onset | Wearable |
| Duration | Wearable |
| Consistency | Wearable |

<Aside type="tip" title="Rationale">
- **Measures:** REM, deep sleep, onset latency, total duration, and consistency are the load-bearing sleep metrics. They predict next-day cognitive and athletic performance and long-run healthspan.
- **Vendor:** Any modern wearable ([Oura](https://ouraring.com/), [Whoop](https://www.whoop.com/), [Eightsleep](https://www.eightsleep.com/), Apple Watch, Garmin) tracks these continuously. Eightsleep adds thermal regulation; Oura is the smallest form factor; Whoop best for athletes.
- **Frequency:** Continuous. Review weekly trend lines, not single nights — one bad night is noise.
</Aside>

## Gap

The first problem is that good sleep is invisible. Your feelings aren't calibrated to know whether your sleep today was better than 100 days ago. Because of this we don't even notice what we are missing out on.

The studies of our own inability to measure ourselves when sleep deprived are well proven. Those who are sleep deprived do not believe they are degraded even though they drive as bad as someone a few beers in.

Nearly any residential bedroom you walk into will fail on:

- **Air** — toxic offgassing from furnishings, blankets, and pillows ([Air › Problem](/habitat/systems/air/#problem))
- **CO2** — 1,500–3,000 ppm buildup overnight behind a closed door
- **Temp** — no targetted regulation overnight
- **Sound** — traffic, barking dogs, pet nails clacking on hardwood
- **Light** — porch lights, streetlights, device LEDs

## Solution

A bedroom is a dark cave you go to sleep in. It doesn't need expansive windows that your neighbor's porch light shines through at night, and that barking dog penetrates. It needs to be the perfect temp, pitch black, perfect air, and sound proof.

First, we have to track sleep quality, and we should see our fundamental aging in LINK_TO_YOU go up as our sleep scores go up.

Then, to create an optimal environment we can achieve the following targets:

- clean textiles LINK TO THE TEXTILES system
- **Air** — hit the [canonical air targets](/habitat/systems/air/#goal). Two loops: 
  - **filtration** remove particles & chemicals and 
  - **fresh-air** Filter alone is not enough; CO2 climbs to 1,500–3,000 ppm overnight in a closed room regardless. (HRV/ERV ideal, cracked window or open door as fallback — dilutes CO2 and VOCs that filters can't). 
- **Temp** — 65–68°F (18–20°C) at body surface; direct cooling beats ambient control.
- **Sound** — ≤30 dB ambient at pillow. Deaden discordant noise or mask with pink noise. See [Systems › Sound](/habitat/systems/sound/).
- **Light** — ≤1 lux at pillow across all wavelengths. See [Systems › Light](/habitat/systems/light/) and [Systems › Circadian](/habitat/systems/circadian/).
