## Problem

We know our caveman biology did not evolve the ability to deal with:
- nanoplastics
- industrial chemicals
- artificial light
- billion $ algorithms and AI hijacks
- static positions from use of screens, keyboards, mice, desks, etc.

Because of that our biggest challenge is we are not even aware of the hidden costs taxing us everyday.

We need to find a way to observe what is really going on so you can factor it in. 

## Goal

1. 360 Awareness: If we were completely aware of how our bodies are being exploited, then we would know what we were losing out on and thus make informed decisions. If you look at the fluffy microfiber pillow and think, sure it's soft, but it's also nudging me towards dementia and a heart attack then you would "price in" those costs.

2. Optimal defaults: If we had a set of systems and rules that ensured the invisible couldn't touch us without express permission, they would effectively cease to be a threat at all. For example, automatic water and air filtration.

## Gap

1. Our body is a mystery, it doesn't have internal diagnostics that can say, "Hey we are 10% dumber than two years ago, I think it's all this plastic in our brain mucking things up". 

2. Setting optimal defaults is overwhelming. You have to understand how biology works, the key threats, the key approaches, and navigate all the noise to find the stuff that actually works. For example, your clothes may be made out of 100% cotton but covered in chemical contaminants from the dying, bleaching, processing, and packaging. Often these contaminants don't just "wash out", they leach out for years.

## Solution

Our bodies take external input from:
- breathing
- swallowing
- skin contact
- injection
- radiation

And internal input from motion and thinking.

Our overall strategy is to:
1. Know what you are made of genetically
2. Measure your "age" so we can "see" progress and problems
2. Make external inputs optimal by default
2. Change internal input to be optimal aka behavior

While changing behavior can be most powerful, modifying the environment is more so because it makes behavior automatic. You could control your brownie bingeing or you could just not have brownies available. Besides, it'll be easier to change when you are no longer being hammered by pollutants.

### Measures
The single best measure is your projected remaining lifespan given your current biology. Why? If you look, act, and perform like a young person then you are a young person. 

This is what we will talk about in the system for this page.

### External inputs

To create optimal defaults, all the tools, and all the information exist, what we really need is simply a curated guide from someone trustworthy. Like this manual!

This will be covered by the "habitat" sections.

### Internal Inputs

Likewise, optimal behaviors and rhythms to run them on, generally exist and we need a curated guide, which will be covered in the "you" section.

## System

The measurement stack, grouped by what it tells you:

- **Baseline** — what you're made of (genetics).
- **Biology** — how you're aging (omics, microbiome, pollution load).
- **Function** — how you're performing (mechanical, emotional, sleep, cognitive).

### Genetic Baseline

You first need to know what you're made of. Whole genome sequencing is a one-time test that lets you tailor interventions and flag personal risks.

| Measure | Method | Vendor | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Whole Genome | DNA sequencing | Nebula | Once (lifetime) |

**Why:** uncovers hidden risk factors and genetic constraints that shape every other intervention downstream.

### Overall Aging

| Measure | Method | Vendor | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Biological Age | Multi-omic epigenetic clock | TruAge | Annual |
| Pace of Aging | DunedinPACE | TruAge | Annual |

- **Why Biological Age?** See where you are — your body's *current* age, not your calendar age.
- **Why Pace?** See where you're going — your aging *trajectory* over the last year.

### Metabolomics

| Measure | Method | Vendor | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Standard blood panel | Blood draw | Ulta Lab Tests | Quarterly |
| Epigenetic metabolomics | Saliva methylation | TruHealth | Annual |

**Why two tests?** Ulta measures current values directly (noisy snapshot). TruHealth infers stable trends from methylation patterns. View them in tandem — a single blood draw has noise; epigenetics smooths it.

### Microbiome

| Measure | Method | Vendor | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Gut composition + diversity | Stool sequencing | Biomesight | Quarterly to annual |

**Why:** everything that enters you is filtered by trillions of bacterial symbiotes. Low diversity → leaky gut, leaky skin, systemic inflammation. You cannot discuss health without considering the microbiome; these bacteria are the gateway to the body.

### Pollution Load

| Measure | Method | Vendor | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| PFAS, heavy metals, microplastics | Blood and urine | TruHealth, PlasticTox, Million Marker | Annual |

**Why:** microplastics accumulate in the brain and don't leave. Blood concentrations rose ~50% in eight years. Annual measurement catches trends before they become permanent.

### Emotional State

| Measure | Method | Target | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Smile frequency | Habitat observation / journaling | 10+ genuine smiles/day | Continuous |
| Play time | Self-log | 30+ min/day | Continuous |
| Positive social contacts | Self-log | 3+ in-person conversations/day | Continuous |

**Why:** emotion is the observable output of a functioning nervous system. Sustained low affect predicts accelerated aging, inflammation, and reduced immune response.

### Cognitive Age

| Measure | Method | Target | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Reaction time | Human Benchmark or similar | Top-quartile for age | Monthly |
| Working memory | Dual N-Back | N ≥ 4 at 70%+ accuracy | Monthly |
| Executive function | Stroop or MoCA | MoCA ≥ 28/30 | Annual |

The challenge: repeatable tasks where practice effects don't dominate your trend. Rotate tests to minimize learning, and pay more attention to *direction* than absolute scores.

## Deploy

Set a cadence and stick to it. Without discipline, measurement becomes noise.

| Cadence | Tests |
| --- | --- |
| Once (lifetime) | Whole genome |
| Annual | TruAge, Pace of Aging, TruHealth epigenetic, pollution load, MoCA, posture |
| Quarterly | Standard blood panel, microbiome, body composition, grip strength |
| Monthly | Reaction time, Dual N-Back |
| Continuous | Sleep, VO2max, RHR, HRV, movement, emotional state |

Store all results in one place (spreadsheet, Notion, Obsidian) with date, value, and reference range per row. Review year-over-year on your birthday: compare pace-of-aging deltas against what you changed in the habitat that year.

**Budget:** ~$500 one-time for whole genome; ~$1,500–3,000/year for annual + quarterly combined; ~$300/year for a wearable. Everything else is time.
