## Problem

We know our caveman biology did not evolve the ability to deal with:
- nanoplastics
- industrial chemicals
- artificial light
- billion $ algorithms and AI hijacks
- static positions from use of screens, keyboards, mice, desks, etc.

Because of that our biggest challenge is we are not even aware of the hidden costs taxing us everyday.

We need to find a way to observe what is really going on so you can factor it in. 

## Goal

1. 360 Awareness: If we were completely aware of how our bodies are being exploited, then we would know what we were losing out on and thus make informed decisions. If you look at the fluffy microfiber pillow and think, sure it's soft, but it's also nudging me towards dementia and a heart attack then you would "price in" those costs.

2. Optimal defaults: If we had a set of systems and rules that ensured the invisible couldn't touch us without express permission, they would effectively cease to be a threat at all. For example, automatic water and air filtration.

## Gap

1. Our body is a mystery, it doesn't have internal diagnostics that can say, "Hey we are 10% dumber than two years ago, I think it's all this plastic in our brain mucking things up". 

2. Setting optimal defaults is overwhelming. You have to understand how biology works, the key threats, the key approaches, and navigate all the noise to find the stuff that actually works. For example, your clothes may be made out of 100% cotton but covered in chemical contaminants from the dying, bleaching, processing, and packaging. Often these contaminants don't just "wash out", they leach out for years.

## Solution

Our bodies take external input from:
- breathing
- swallowing
- skin contact
- injection
- radiation

And internal input from motion and thinking.

Our overall strategy is to:
1. Know what you are made of genetically
2. Measure your "age" so we can "see" progress and problems
3. Make external inputs optimal by default
4. Change internal input to be optimal aka behavior

While changing behavior can be most powerful, modifying the environment is more so because it makes behavior automatic. You could control your brownie bingeing or you could just not have brownies available. Besides, it'll be easier to change when you are no longer being hammered by pollutants.

### Measures
The single best measure is your projected remaining lifespan given your current biology. Why? If you look, act, and perform like a young person then you are a young person. 

This is what we will talk about in the system for this page.

### External inputs

To create optimal defaults, all the tools, and all the information exist, what we really need is simply a curated guide from someone trustworthy. Like this manual!

This will be covered by [Habitat](/habitat/) sections.

### Internal Inputs

Likewise, optimal behaviors and rhythms to run them on generally exist and we need a curated guide. Behaviors are nested under each [Space](/habitat/spaces/bedroom/); rhythms start with [Dailies](/you/rhythms/dailies/).

## System

Here are the key measures that are worth using today.

### Genetic Baseline

You first need to know what you're made of. Whole genome sequencing is a one-time test that lets you tailor interventions and flag personal risks.

| Measure | Method | Vendor | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Whole Genome | DNA sequencing | [Nebula](https://nebula.org/) | Once (lifetime) |

<Aside type="tip" title="Rationale">
- **Measure:** Whole genome sequencing surfaces personal risk factors (e.g., APOE for Alzheimer's risk, BRCA1/2 for cancer, MTHFR for methylation) that determine which downstream interventions matter most. Foundational for everything else.
- **Vendor:** [Nebula](https://nebula.org/) offers 30x whole-genome sequencing (vs 23andMe's much smaller SNP array) with portable data and no third-party data sales.
- **Frequency:** Once. Your germline DNA doesn't change.
</Aside>

**Why:** uncovers hidden risk factors and genetic constraints that shape every other intervention downstream.

### Epigenetic Age

| Measure | Method | Vendor | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Biological Age | Multi-omic epigenetic clock | [TruAge](https://www.trudiagnostic.com/) | Annual |
| Pace of Aging | DunedinPACE | [TruAge](https://www.trudiagnostic.com/) | Annual |

<Aside type="tip" title="Rationale">
- **Measures:** Biological Age = your body's *current* age via methylation patterns. Pace of Aging = your aging *velocity* — how fast you're aging right now. [DunedinPACE](https://elifesciences.org/articles/73420) is the most validated longitudinal pace clock.
- **Vendor:** [TruDiagnostic](https://www.trudiagnostic.com/) is the leading commercial provider of validated epigenetic clocks; partnered with the original Duke researchers behind DunedinPACE.
- **Frequency:** Annual. Methylation patterns change slowly; sub-annual measurement is mostly noise.
</Aside>

- **Why Biological Age?** See where you are — your body's *current* age, not your calendar age.
- **Why Pace?** See where you're going — your aging *trajectory* over the last year. DunedinPACE is the most validated of the pace clocks.[^1]

### Metabolomics

| Measure | Method | Vendor | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Standard blood panel | Blood draw | [Ulta Lab Tests](https://www.ultalabtests.com/) | Quarterly |
| Epigenetic metabolomics | Saliva methylation | [TruHealth](https://www.trudiagnostic.com/) | Annual |

<Aside type="tip" title="Rationale">
- **Measures:** Standard blood panel = current snapshot (glucose, lipids, hormones, inflammation, organ markers). Epigenetic metabolomics = the same domains inferred from stable methylation patterns; smooths out daily noise so you see trend, not fluctuation.
- **Vendor:** [Ulta Lab Tests](https://www.ultalabtests.com/) — direct-to-consumer, no doctor referral required, ~half the price of typical hospital labs. [TruHealth](https://www.trudiagnostic.com/) for the epigenetic complement (often bundled with TruAge).
- **Frequency:** Quarterly for blood (rapidly responsive to diet, sleep, training). Annual for epigenetic (slow-moving baseline).
</Aside>

**Why two tests?** Ulta measures current values directly (noisy snapshot). TruHealth infers stable trends from methylation patterns. View them in tandem — a single blood draw has noise; epigenetics smooths it.

### Microbiome

| Measure | Method | Vendor | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Gut composition + diversity | Stool sequencing | [Biomesight](https://biomesight.com/) | Quarterly to annual |

<Aside type="tip" title="Rationale">
- **Measure:** Gut composition + diversity. Low diversity predicts gut barrier dysfunction ("leaky gut"), systemic inflammation, and weakened immunity. Trillions of bacterial cells filter every food and drug input you take.
- **Vendor:** [Biomesight](https://biomesight.com/) — 16S rRNA sequencing with high-resolution taxonomic reporting, longitudinal trend tracking, and open data export.
- **Frequency:** Quarterly to annual. Microbiome shifts within weeks of dietary change; quarterly catches intervention effects.
</Aside>

**Why:** everything that enters you is filtered by trillions of bacterial symbiotes. It's part of your body. Low diversity → leaky gut, leaky skin, systemic inflammation. 

### Pollution Load

| Measure | Method | Vendor | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| PFAS, heavy metals, microplastics | Blood | [TruHealth](https://www.trudiagnostic.com/), PlasticTox, [Million Marker](https://www.millionmarker.com/) | Annual |

<Aside type="tip" title="Rationale">
- **Measures:** PFAS, heavy metals, and microplastics bioaccumulate in tissues over years and predict elevated risk for cancer, neurodegeneration, and reproductive harm. Brain microplastic concentrations rose ~50% from 2016 to 2024 ([Nihart 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-024-03453-1)).
- **Vendor:** [TruHealth](https://www.trudiagnostic.com/) bundles a pollution panel; [Million Marker](https://www.millionmarker.com/) and PlasticTox specialize in microplastic and endocrine-disruptor measurement.
- **Frequency:** Annual. Detoxification is slow; tighter intervals don't yield actionable signal.
</Aside>

**Why:** pollutants accumulate in the body and are a form of aging. Brain microplastic concentrations rose ~50% between 2016 and 2024.[^2] As your body becomes more polluted your longevity decreases.

### Mechanical Age

| Measure | Method | Target | Cadence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Body composition | DEXA or smart scale | ≤20% body fat (men), ≤28% (women); top-quartile skeletal muscle index | Quarterly |
| VO2 max | Wearable or lab | Top-quartile for age | Continuous |
| Resting HR | Wearable | ≤60 bpm | Continuous |
| HRV | Wearable | >60 ms (age-adjusted) | Continuous |
| Movement volume | Wearable | 10k+ steps/day | Continuous |
| Movement intensity | Wearable | Zone 2 ≥3h/week + VO2max work ≥30min/week | Weekly |
| Movement variety | Wearable + log | 4+ modalities/week | Weekly |
| Grip strength | Hand dynamometer | Top-quartile for age | Quarterly |
| Posture | `TODO` — DEXA alignment or PosturePro | Neutral curve, no forward head | Annual |

<Aside type="tip" title="Rationale">
- **Measures:** Cardiorespiratory fitness in the top quartile is associated with roughly half the all-cause mortality of the bottom quartile ([Mandsager 2018](https://doi.org/10.1001/jamanetworkopen.2018.3605)). Step count above ~7,000/day cuts mortality risk substantially ([Saint-Maurice 2020](https://doi.org/10.1001/jama.2020.1382)). HRV reflects autonomic recovery; grip strength is a robust sarcopenia screen; body composition tracks metabolic health.
- **Vendor:** Modern wearables (Oura, Whoop, Apple Watch, Garmin) cover continuous measures. [DEXA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual-energy_X-ray_absorptiometry) at a clinic for body-composition gold standard. Hand dynamometers are cheap and accurate for grip.
- **Frequency:** Continuous for wearable measures (HR, HRV, sleep, movement). Quarterly for body composition + grip strength. Annual for DEXA + posture.
</Aside>

**Why:** life is motion, if you cannot move, you cannot live. Cardiorespiratory fitness in the top quartile is associated with roughly half the all-cause mortality of the bottom quartile.[^3]
